Hi! I'm Kecen Pu 蒲柯岑.

I am a second-year master's student studying psychology at Teachers College, Columbia University. Before coming to NYC, I received Bachelor of Science in Psychology at Beijing Normal University.

My interest in psychology started when I was a 15-year-old teenager. I would never get tired of observing people. Their face, body movements, even the tiniest subconscious reaction kept me wondering: What would be the reason?

During my academic career, I've had the priviledge to be involved in different research projects. If you are interested, please click my CV.

Currently, my research interest lies in the influence of input language on simple mathematical calculation tasks in Mandarin-English speakers. I use EEG and behavioral tasks to investigate into the question. I'm also fascinated by the cognitive mechanisms underpinning complex mental processes, such as decision making.

Embarked on a journey to become a scientist-practitioner, every success and failure that I've encountered reminds me of persistence and resilience. As the line in Dead Poets Society goes, "Carpe diem. Seize the day!"
